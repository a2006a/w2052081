Agroecology

Agroecology is a farming method that aims to support sustainable agriculture by imitating natural ecosystems. It places special emphasis on biodiversity, soil health, and ecological balance while highlighting the connection of plants, animals, and the environment.


Agroecology is the application of ecological concepts to agricultural methods, such as crop rotation, polyculture, and agroforestry, to increase soil fertility, lessen the impact of pests and diseases, and strengthen resilience to climate change.


Agroecology farmers use natural processes to grow food sustainably and with the least amount of negative environmental impact possible. By supporting local food systems, enabling small-scale farmers, and conserving cultural traditions and traditional knowledge, agroecology also promotes social and economic fairness.
