African Elephant

Habitat:African elephants inhabit savannas, forests, and grasslands across sub-Saharan Africa.

BehaviorThey are know for their social behavior, living in herds led by matriarchs.

Diet:African elephantsare herbivores, feedingon a variety of vegetation including grasses, leaves, and fruits

Conservation Status:African elephants are listed as vulnerabledue to habitat loss, paching, and human-wildlife conflict.

Redwood Tree

Habitat:Redwood Trees are native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon in the United States.

BehaviorThey are among the tallest and longest-lived treespecies, with some individuals reaching over 300 feet in heightand living for thousnadsof years.

Diet:Redwood trees are photosynthetic organisms, obtainingnutrients through photosynthesis.

Conservation Status:Redwood trees are classified as least concern, but they face threats fromlogging, urbanization, and climate change

Bengal Tiger

Habitat:Bengal tigers are found primarily in the forests of India, Bangladesh,Nepal, Bhutan, and Myanmar.

BehaviorThey are solitary and territorial animals, with males having larger home rangesthan females.

Diet:Bengal tigers are carnivores, preying on a variety of animals including deer, wild boar, and water buffalo

Conservation Status:Bengal tiger are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and human enroachment.

Yoshino Cherry

Habitat:Zones 5-8, from the West Coast to the East Coast, except for the coldest and hottest climates. Regarded as native to Japan

BehaviorThey lose their leaves in the winter, typically bloom in early spring. The flowering period is relativly short lasting about 2 -3 weeks

Diet:Doesn't consume food in the traditional sense, they do undergo photosynthesis to produce energy

Conservation Status:Wildy cultivated and are not considered endangered They face threats from habitat loss, disease, and pests Conservation efforts often focus on botanical gardens and other ciltivated settings.
